Sticks, Stones, and Marrowed Bones

May 8, 2025
Axiom Deconstruction
Healing
Transformation
🔥 The Phoenix Prism

You are standing at the mouth of something ancient—maybe myth, maybe memory, maybe both. This is a story you already know, carried in bruised childhood sayings, hollow silences, and dreams you didn't know how to remember. Here, we do not rush. There is no timer. No test. No need to perform the shape of healing. You do not need to believe anything yet. You do not even need to agree. All that is needed now is a slight leaning forward, a tilt of the soul toward something different—something alive.

"Sticks and stones may break my bones, but words will never hurt me."

This was never a shield. It was a muzzle. A survival phrase born in a time where silence was safer than honesty. Whispered to children across generations, taught in schoolyards, repeated in moments of pain—this simple phrase has become embedded in our collective consciousness...

If these words have ever been used to invalidate your pain or justify someone else's harm, you are not alone. They've been weaponized, recycled, etched into cartoons, classrooms, courtrooms. For many of us, it became a necessary tool for survival, a way to move through hostile environments without shattering under their weight...
